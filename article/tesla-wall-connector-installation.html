Before You Start: Electrical Requirements

The Tesla Wall Connector is a hardwired EVSE that requires a dedicated circuit. Here's what you need to verify before purchasing or installing:

Panel Capacity Check

Your electrical panel needs sufficient capacity to add a 60A circuit (for 48A charging) or at minimum a 50A circuit (for 40A charging). Check your panel for:

  • Available breaker slots: You need 2 adjacent slots for a double-pole breaker
  • Total panel amperage: Most homes have 100A or 200A service. 200A handles EV charging easily; 100A may require a load calculation
  • Current load: Add up existing breakers to estimate headroom

Step-by-Step Installation

1

Obtain Permits

Most jurisdictions require an electrical permit for EV charger installation. Contact your local building department‚Äîpermits typically cost $50-150 and ensure your work is inspected for safety.

2

Plan the Wire Run

Map the shortest path from your electrical panel to the charger location. Consider interior vs. exterior routing, conduit requirements, and future serviceability. Tesla recommends mounting 48" from floor to bottom of unit.

3

Install the Circuit Breaker

Turn off the main breaker and verify power is off with a voltage tester. Install the 60A double-pole breaker in your panel. Connect hot wires to the breaker, neutral to the neutral bar, and ground to the ground bar.

4

Run the Wiring

Pull wire through conduit or along your planned route. Leave 18-24" of extra wire at both ends. Secure conduit every 4 feet and within 12" of boxes.

5

Mount the Wall Connector

Use the included mounting bracket on a solid surface‚Äîstuds for drywall, or appropriate anchors for masonry. Route wires through the back knockout or bottom entry point.

6

Make Connections

Inside the Wall Connector, connect the two hot wires to L1 and L2 terminals, and ground wire to the ground terminal. Note: Gen 3 does NOT require a neutral wire.

7

Set Amperage & Test

Set the internal rotary dial to match your breaker rating. Turn on the breaker‚Äîthe LED should illuminate. Use the Tesla app to complete WiFi setup and verify charging.

Common Installation Issues

LED Shows Red or No Light

Red indicates a fault. Check all wire connections are tight and correctly positioned. Verify the breaker is on and providing 240V between hots.

WiFi Connection Failed

The Wall Connector supports 2.4GHz networks only. Ensure your router is within range or consider a WiFi extender.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I install the Wall Connector myself?
Legally, it depends on your jurisdiction. Many areas allow homeowners to do their own electrical work with a permit and inspection. If you're not comfortable working in an electrical panel, hire a licensed electrician‚Äîtypically $300-800 for labor.
Does the Wall Connector work with non-Tesla EVs?
Yes! With a Tesla to J1772 adapter, it can charge any EV using the J1772 standard. However, most J1772 vehicles cap at 32A.
Can I install this outdoors?
Yes, the Wall Connector is rated for outdoor installation (NEMA 3R equivalent). It operates from -22¬∞F to 122¬∞F.
