Design and Build Quality

The Home Flex features a compact, rounded design that looks more refined than industrial competitors. The matte gray housing measures 14.5" x 7" x 5.5" and weighs 18 pounds—substantial enough to feel premium but not unwieldy for installation.

Build quality is excellent. The housing is NEMA 4 rated for outdoor installation, meaning it handles rain, snow, and dust without issue. We've had ours mounted outside for 18 months through harsh winters with zero problems.

The 23-foot charging cable is a standout feature—longer than most competitors and sufficient to reach any parking orientation. The cable is also thicker and more flexible than budget chargers, resisting tangles in cold weather.

Installation Options

ChargePoint offers unmatched installation flexibility:

Plug-In Installation

The Home Flex can plug into a NEMA 14-50 or NEMA 6-50 outlet using included adapters. This makes installation simple and allows you to take the charger if you move. Just be aware the maximum output is 40A continuous on a 50A circuit (per NEC 80% rule).

Hardwired Installation

For maximum power, hardwire the unit to a 60A breaker for full 50A/12kW charging. This delivers about 37 miles of range per hour for most EVs.

Amperage Flexibility

Here's where the Home Flex shines: you can set the charging amperage via the app to match your circuit. Options include 16A, 24A, 32A, 40A, and 50A. This means you can start with a lower-capacity circuit and upgrade later without replacing the charger.

App and Smart Features

The ChargePoint app is where this charger justifies its premium price. Features include:

Charging Schedules

Set up to 4 weekly schedules to charge during off-peak electricity rates. The app shows your utility's time-of-use rates (if available) and estimates cost per charge.

Charging History

View detailed logs of every session including duration, energy delivered, cost, and carbon offset. Export data for expense reports or tracking.

Reminders

Get notified if you forget to plug in, when charging completes, or if there's an issue. Customizable and genuinely useful.

Voice Control

Works with Amazon Alexa and Google Assistant. "Alexa, start charging" actually works reliably.

ChargePoint Network Integration

Your home charger integrates with the ChargePoint public network. You can see all charging sessions (home and public) in one app and manage payment methods for public stations.

WiFi Required: The Home Flex requires WiFi for setup and all smart features. Without WiFi, it still charges your EV but you lose scheduling, history, and remote control. Ensure you have reliable WiFi in your garage or charging location.

Real-World Charging Performance

We tested the Home Flex with multiple vehicles:

  • Tesla Model 3 Long Range: Charged at full 48A (11.5 kW) with a J1772 adapter. Added 34 miles of range per hour. Full charge (20% to 90%) completed in about 6 hours.
  • Ford Mustang Mach-E: Accepted full 48A charge rate. Delivered 32 miles of range per hour due to slightly lower efficiency.
  • Chevrolet Bolt EUV: Maxed out at 32A (the Bolt's onboard charger limit), adding about 25 miles per hour.

The charger delivered consistent performance across hundreds of sessions with no failures or connectivity issues. The LED indicator provides clear status feedback, and the holster keeps the connector off the ground when not in use.

Installation Costs

The charger itself is $699, but total installed cost depends on your electrical situation:

  • If you have an existing NEMA 14-50 outlet: $699 total—just plug and play
  • New outlet installation: Add $300-700 for electrician labor plus materials
  • Hardwired installation: Add $400-900 for a new 60A circuit
  • Panel upgrade needed: Could add $1,500-4,000 for 100A to 200A upgrade

Many utility companies and states offer rebates of $200-500 for home EV charger installation. Check the DSIRE database for incentives in your area.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the ChargePoint Home Flex work with Tesla?
Yes, with a J1772 to Tesla adapter. Newer Teslas (2024+) with the NACS port need a NACS to J1772 adapter. The Home Flex will charge any Tesla at up to 48A, which is nearly as fast as Tesla's own Wall Connector.
Is the ChargePoint Home Flex worth it over cheaper chargers?
If you value smart features, build quality, and flexibility, yes. Budget chargers work fine for basic charging, but the Home Flex offers better app integration, adjustable amperage, and longer cable. The 3-year warranty also provides peace of mind.
Can I install ChargePoint Home Flex myself?
If you're using the plug-in option with an existing outlet, yes. For hardwired installation, we recommend a licensed electrician unless you're experienced with electrical work. Some jurisdictions require professional installation for permit approval.
Does ChargePoint work without internet?
The charger will function and charge your EV without internet, but you lose all smart features: scheduling, history, remote control, and notifications. WiFi is required for initial setup.
What's the difference between ChargePoint Home Flex and Flex 2?
As of early 2025, the "Flex" and "Home Flex" are the same product. ChargePoint hasn't released a Flex 2. If you see different naming, it's typically regional variations or old stock.
