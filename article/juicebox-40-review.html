JuiceBox 40 Review: Smart Charging at a Competitive Price

Updated February 2025 13 min read Hands-On Review

The JuiceBox 40 from Enel X Way has been a popular choice for EV owners seeking smart features without the premium price of ChargePoint. After a year of testing, here's our complete assessment of whether it deserves a spot in your garage.

Quick Summary

The JuiceBox 40 delivers excellent value for smart EV charging. It offers most of the features of premium chargers at a lower price point, with standout capabilities like utility integration for time-of-use optimization and a user-friendly app. The trade-off is slightly lower build quality and a 40A maximum versus 50A competitors.

Best For: Budget-conscious EV owners who want smart features. Excellent choice if your EV's onboard charger maxes out at 32A (like most non-Tesla EVs) since you won't miss the extra 10A of higher-capacity units.

Design and Build

The JuiceBox 40 features a distinctive curved design with an LED display panel showing charging status, power output, and WiFi connectivity. The housing is predominantly plastic, which keeps costs down but doesn't feel as robust as the metal ChargePoint or Tesla units.

Dimensions are reasonable at 18" x 7" x 4", and at 15 pounds it's lighter than most competitors. The NEMA 4 rating means it handles outdoor installation in rain and snow, though we'd recommend a sheltered location if possible to protect the LED panel.

The standout physical feature is the 25-foot cable—the longest of any major smart charger. This extra length is genuinely useful for garages with awkward layouts or driveways where you park in different spots.

Installation

Plug-In Option

The JuiceBox 40 can plug into a NEMA 14-50 outlet using the included adapter. This limits continuous charging to 32A (per the 80% rule on 40A circuits), which is actually perfect since the JuiceBox 40 maxes out at 40A anyway. If you have a 50A circuit, you're effectively using 32A of capacity.

Hardwired Installation

For full 40A output, hardwire to a 50A breaker. The internal terminal block accepts up to 6 AWG wire. Installation is straightforward for anyone comfortable with electrical work, though we always recommend permits and inspections.

JuiceNet App Experience

The JuiceNet app is where the JuiceBox shines. Enel X (the parent company) specializes in grid services, and that expertise shows:

Smart Charging Features

  • Time-of-Use Optimization: Automatically schedules charging during cheapest electricity rates
  • Utility Integration: Works with over 300 utilities to pull real-time rates
  • Target Charge Level: Set your desired battery percentage and departure time
  • Green Charging: Prioritize charging when renewable energy is on the grid

Monitoring and Control

  • Real-time charging status and power output
  • Complete charging history with cost tracking
  • Push notifications for charging events
  • Remote start/stop capability
  • Amperage adjustment (16A-40A)

Smart Home Integration

The JuiceBox works with Amazon Alexa and Google Assistant for voice control. Commands like "Alexa, start my JuiceBox" work reliably after setup.

WiFi Setup Issues: Some users report difficulty with initial WiFi pairing, especially on 5GHz networks (the JuiceBox only supports 2.4GHz). If you have issues, try temporarily disabling your 5GHz band or moving closer to the router during setup.

Charging Performance

Our testing with multiple vehicles showed consistent performance:

  • Tesla Model Y: 32A charging with J1772 adapter. Added 25 miles of range per hour. (Tesla can accept 48A, but the JuiceBox 40 caps at 40A.)
  • Hyundai Ioniq 5: Full 40A charging rate. Delivered approximately 29 miles of range per hour—excellent for this vehicle.
  • Nissan Leaf Plus: Maxed out at the Leaf's 6.6kW limit (27A), adding about 22 miles per hour.

The JuiceBox 40 handled all vehicles without issue. Power delivery was consistent, and the charger recovered gracefully from power outages and WiFi disconnections.

Reliability and Support

Over a year of daily use, we experienced no hardware failures. The charger reliably started sessions, maintained power output, and survived temperature extremes from 10°F to 95°F.

WiFi connectivity was stable after initial setup, with the charger reconnecting automatically after router reboots or brief internet outages.

Customer support is a mixed bag. Enel X Way (formerly Enel X) has gone through corporate restructuring, and some users report slow response times. The product itself is solid, but set expectations accordingly for support needs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does JuiceBox 40 work with Tesla?
Yes, with a J1772 to Tesla adapter (not included). The JuiceBox 40 will charge any Tesla at up to 40A (9.6 kW). For newer Teslas with NACS ports, you'll need a NACS to J1772 adapter.
Can I use JuiceBox 40 without WiFi?
Yes, the charger works without WiFi for basic charging. However, you'll lose all smart features including scheduling, history, and the utility rate integration that makes this charger special. WiFi is required for initial setup.
Is JuiceBox owned by Enel X or Enel X Way?
Enel X rebranded to Enel X Way in 2022 for their EV charging division. JuiceBox products are now sold under Enel X Way, but you'll still see "Enel X" branding on some units and documentation. It's the same company.
What's the difference between JuiceBox 32 and 40?
The JuiceBox 32 is discontinued but offered 32A max charging. The JuiceBox 40 offers 40A max. If you find a discounted JuiceBox 32, it's fine for most EVs since many cap at 32A anyway. But for new purchases, get the 40.
Does JuiceBox qualify for tax credits or rebates?
The JuiceBox is Energy Star certified, making it eligible for many utility rebates. Federal EV charger tax credits (up to 30% of cost including installation) may apply depending on your location. Check the IRS guidelines and your local utility for specific incentives.

Final Verdict

The JuiceBox 40 earns a solid recommendation as a value-oriented smart charger. It delivers 90% of the ChargePoint Home Flex experience at a lower price, with the best utility integration of any home charger we've tested.

Choose the JuiceBox 40 if you want smart features without breaking the bank, especially if your EV's onboard charger maxes out at 32A or you want to take advantage of time-of-use electricity rates.

Consider the ChargePoint Home Flex instead if you need 50A charging, prefer metal construction, or want the slightly more polished app experience.
